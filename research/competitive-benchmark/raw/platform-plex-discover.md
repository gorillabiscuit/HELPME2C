# Plex Discover — competitive benchmark (raw notes)

**Scope of this writeup:** Plex Discover is the streaming-aggregator / "what to watch across all your services" surface inside Plex, distinct from the classic Plex Media Server (the self-hosted library product). Discover is what competes with JustWatch / Reelgood and what most of the social and recommendation work has been built on top of since 2022. Where relevant we also cover *Discover Together* (the friend-based social layer added late 2023) and *Watch Together* (the older co-viewing feature, now being sunset on most platforms — see §3).

Time range: sources from 2022-04 onward, with most weight on 2023-04 (Vionlabs partnership), 2023-11 (Discover Together public debut + backlash) and 2024-05 (Plex Pro Week + Index Exchange interview). Today's date: 2026-05-17.

---

## 1. Signup signals

Plex account signup itself is **deliberately thin**. Per [Plex's Sign Up page](https://www.plex.tv/sign-up/) and the [Plex Accounts support article](https://support.plex.tv/articles/201862428-plex-accounts/):

- You can sign up with **email + password**, **Google**, or **Apple**. (Facebook login was deprecated — see [Yahoo / Plex Ending Facebook Login Support](https://www.yahoo.com/tech/plex-ending-facebook-login-support-204607216.html).)
- **No credit card** required to create a free account.
- A default username is "generated by taking half of the first part of the email address and appending numbers until it's unique" ([Plex Accounts support](https://support.plex.tv/articles/201862428-plex-accounts/) summary).
- No demographic questions (age/region) at the basic signup step. Region is inferred from IP for availability metadata.

What Discover specifically needs you to do *after* signup:

- **Streaming services selection.** Per Plex's [Discover Source support article](https://support.plex.tv/articles/discover/) (404'd on direct fetch, summarised in [HelloTech](https://www.hellotech.com/blog/how-to-add-streaming-service-to-plex) and [HowToGeek](https://www.howtogeek.com/how-i-used-plex-to-unify-all-my-streaming-services/)): you're prompted to tick which streaming services you subscribe to (Netflix, Prime, Disney+, Hulu, Max, Apple TV+, etc). This drives the availability badges and personalises which titles are surfaced. Can be edited later at *Settings > Streaming Services*.
- **Linking a Plex Media Server is optional.** You can use Discover as a pure streaming aggregator with no PMS at all — this is the explicit pivot from "own your library" to "discover across streaming" (see §6 backlash).
- **External IDs:** no required Trakt / TMDB / MAL / AniList linkage at signup. Plex has its own metadata pipeline; Watchlist / ratings live in your Plex account directly.

Quote from [HowToGeek](https://www.howtogeek.com/how-i-used-plex-to-unify-all-my-streaming-services/) via secondary summary: *"During setup, users can select their favorite streaming services (like Hulu, Netflix, Max, and Disney+) to see what's trending and discover more."*

## 2. Cold-start UX

For a brand-new Plex Discover user, the first session is shaped by:

1. **Streaming services picker.** Per [Plex Discover Source support](https://support.plex.tv/articles/discover/): *"You'll be taken through an initial quick walkthrough when first launching an app, if you haven't already selected your services. You can always adjust them later, from your app or Plex account settings."* If skipped, the user can add services later from Settings.
2. **No genre / mood / "pick 5 shows you like" cold-start.** None of the secondary coverage ([Beginner's Guide to Plex](https://www.plex.tv/blog/a-beginners-guide-to-plex/), [HowToGeek](https://www.howtogeek.com/how-i-used-plex-to-unify-all-my-streaming-services/), [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/plex-discovery-searches-netflix-disney-hbo-max-yout-1848822252)) describes a taste-elicitation picker à la Netflix's 3-show onboarding or Letterboxd's "rate your favourites". The cold-start signal is **streaming services you subscribe to**, period.
3. **Discover Together onboarding screens** — added late 2023. This is the screen where users implicitly opt themselves into watch-history sharing (see §6). Per [PCGamer's coverage](https://www.pcgamer.com/plex-says-controversial-service-that-emails-your-anime-habits-to-your-mates-is-opt-in-actually-pointing-at-a-screen-where-you-still-have-to-turn-it-off/) and [TechHive](https://www.techhive.com/article/2157803/plex-discover-together-privacy-concerns.html): *"Privacy settings defaulted from 'Private' to 'Friends Only' for watch history unless manually changed during onboarding."*
4. **Watchlist tab has two modes.** Per the [Universal Watchlist support article](https://support.plex.tv/articles/universal-watchlist/) (summary): a "Recommended" tab sorts your watchlist into hubs (e.g. "available on services you have", "recently added") plus the raw Watchlist tab.

What's notably **absent**:

- No quiz, no "rate these 10 posters", no genre-pickers.
- No anime-specific cold-start step.
- No "who do you watch with?" group-profile cold-start (relevant for HelpME2C's group-rec angle — Plex's group thinking is bolted on later via Discover Together, not designed into onboarding).

## 3. Group-recommendation feature

Plex has **three distinct features** people lump together, and only one of them is plausibly a group-rec surface. Worth separating clearly:

### 3a. Watch Together (co-viewing, sunset on most platforms)

- Launched 2020. Per [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2020/05/28/plex-launches-a-co-watching-experience-for-its-on-demand-library-and-users-personal-media/): synchronised playback with friends — anyone in the session can pause/scrub for everyone.
- Limit: "no official limit … but you are limited by the hardware you're using and the speed of your network connection. Each person watching constitutes a new stream" ([HowToGeek Watch Together guide](https://www.howtogeek.com/888424/how-to-use-the-plex-watch-together-feature/)).
- **Not a recommendation feature** — purely a co-viewing player. You still pick a title yourselves.
- **Being phased out.** Per [Android Authority](https://www.androidauthority.com/plex-watch-together-removed-3529939/) and [PCWorld](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2619590/plex-is-dropping-a-popular-feature-from-its-new-streaming-apps/): as of the Feb 2025 app redesign, Watch Together is removed from most native apps but kept in the web app "for the foreseeable future". Plex left the door open to reintroducing similar functionality with new tooling.

### 3b. Discover Together (social activity feed, 2023)

- Public debut Nov 2023, [Plex's announcement blog](https://www.plex.tv/blog/discover-together/) and [TechCrunch coverage](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/01/plex-becomes-a-social-network-with-public-debut-of-discover-together/).
- Features per [TechHive](https://www.techhive.com/article/2120830/plex-discover-together-news.html): see what friends are streaming/liking/watchlisting, "watch histories, the titles on their watch lists, and their ratings of recently watched TV shows and movies."
- People tab — add friends, see *friend recommendations from Plex based on shared friends*.
- On each content page, "history that shows which friends have watched and rated it, to help you determine if you might like to watch, too" ([Plex blog](https://www.plex.tv/blog/discover-together/)).
- Sharing: pick a friend or **create a group** to share a title with; group members can comment on the shared item ([Plex blog](https://www.plex.tv/blog/discover-together/)).
- **This is the closest Plex gets to group-aware recs**, but in practice it's a social activity feed with a "trending with friends" cohort signal layered on top — not a genuine joint-recommendation engine that takes multiple user profiles and produces a single ranked list optimised for all of them. There's no "we're watching together tonight, recommend something for the three of us" surface.

Plex's own framing ([Index Exchange Q&A](https://www.indexexchange.com/en-gb/2024/05/22/improving-content-discovery-streaming-tv-plex/)): *"This friend activity data will help Plex suggest which shows are trending with friends this week."* That's a **cohort/popularity signal** flavoured by friend graph, not group-rec scoring.

### 3c. Shared library / friends with PMS access

Pre-dates Discover Together. Per [TechHive privacy follow-up](https://www.techhive.com/article/2157803/plex-discover-together-privacy-concerns.html): anyone you had previously granted access to your personal Plex library was **automatically added to your Plex friends list** when Discover Together launched, which is what caused the 2023 backlash (see §6). This conflation of "library share" and "social friend" was retroactively split into separate concepts.

**Net assessment for HelpME2C:** Plex does not ship a true group-recommendation engine. Discover Together gets the closest by surfacing what your social graph is watching, but recommendations are still solo-personalised; the group is a *signal*, not a *target*.

## 4. Cross-medium scoring

Plex's content surface today:

- **Movies and TV (linear)** — core Discover content type.
- **Anime** — covered by Plex's anime hub (see [watch.plex.tv anime category](https://watch.plex.tv/en-GB/discover/category/anime)), but treated as a *genre* of TV/film rather than a separate first-class medium with anime-native taxonomy. There is no MAL/AniList-style staff/studio/seiyū-keyed graph in Discover.
- **Live TV + FAST channels** — present as separate tabs (Live TV, On Demand) ([PCWorld coverage](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2654717/annoyed-by-the-new-plex-app-change-these-4-settings.html)).
- **Podcasts** — **discontinued.** Per [TechCrunch, April 2022](https://techcrunch.com/2022/04/12/plex-pulls-the-plug-on-podcasts-and-web-shows/): *"Plex pulls the plug on podcasts and web shows."* Plex had earlier flagged ambitions to do cross-medium podcast↔TV recs ([Medium, Andrew McGivern](https://medium.com/@podcasthero/podcasts-are-coming-to-plex-and-they-have-big-plans-3b021715df5)) but those never shipped before the wind-down.
- **Music** — Plex has Plexamp / personal music library but it is **not** integrated into Discover recommendation flows the way TV/film are.

**Cross-medium scoring:** essentially **siloed**. Discover surfaces TV and film side by side (because both are "things you watch on a screen tonight"), but there is no public evidence of cross-medium scoring of the kind HelpME2C is targeting (anime↔live-action thematic bridges). The Vionlabs taxonomy (see §5) — moods, predicted genres, descriptive keywords — is medium-internal: it categorises *titles*, not bridges between media.

Could-not-verify: whether Plex's internal "trending" / "for you" rails are scored against unified embeddings spanning anime + live-action + non-English series, or whether each is a separate model. Public docs don't say.

## 5. Recommendation algorithm

Best public source is the May 2024 [Index Exchange Q&A with Plex](https://www.indexexchange.com/en-gb/2024/05/22/improving-content-discovery-streaming-tv-plex/). Direct quotes:

- *"We can now provide tailored recommendations by analysing various signals derived from viewers' behavior, as well as the preferences of their selected networks."*
- *"This fusion of machine learning and human curation enables us to categorise viewers into distinct profiles, facilitating more precise content matching."*
- *"Our app offers customisability that allows viewers to tailor their experience with unique layouts, favourite channels, and recommendations based on both machine learning and human input."*
- On the social signal: *"Viewers are more likely to watch content recommended by people they know and trust, and Plex is the first service to bring streaming and community together in a single platform."*

The underlying ML stack is partly **Vionlabs**, announced April 2023 ([Light Reading](https://www.lightreading.com/ai-machine-learning/plex-taps-vionlabs-to-drive-more-personalization-into-the-streaming-experience), [Vionlabs press release](https://www.prweb.com/releases/2023/04/prweb19263958.htm), [Videonet's writeup with emphasis on mood/emotion](https://www.v-net.tv/2023/04/06/plex-races-to-leading-edge-of-content-discovery-innovation-with-heavy-focus-on-emotion-and-mood/)). What Vionlabs supplies:

- **Fingerprint embeddings** per title (used for similarity).
- **30+ Predicted Genres** layered on top of standard genre tags.
- **20+ AI-extracted Mood categories**, with up to **65+ mood tags per video file**, measured per-scene.
- **1,200+ descriptive keywords** for topic / element tagging.
- Stated goal: connect titles across different genres via mood, build "micro-taste groups".

Plex announced 16M monthly actives at the time of the Vionlabs deal ([Light Reading](https://www.lightreading.com/ai-machine-learning/plex-taps-vionlabs-to-drive-more-personalization-into-the-streaming-experience)).

Other algorithm-adjacent signals documented:

- **Friend graph data** feeds a "trending with friends" hub ([Plex blog](https://www.plex.tv/blog/discover-together/)).
- **Ratings + watchlist + watch history** feed personalised recommendations ([TechCrunch Discover Together](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/01/plex-becomes-a-social-network-with-public-debut-of-discover-together/): *"Recommendations are based on your services, ratings, and now, your friends."*).
- **Streaming services owned** acts as a hard filter (only titles you can actually play are surfaced — at least in theory; see §6 failure mode where this breaks).
- **Sentiment from in-app feedback** is processed by unitQ ([PRNewswire, Plex × unitQ](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/plex-chooses-unitq-artificial-intelligence-to-gauge-sentiment-bolster-user-experience-301771647.html)), but that's QA / product-feedback AI, not the rec model.

Plex Pro Week 2024 included a Discover-focused session per the Plex Forum thread ([Plex Pros 2024](https://forums.plex.tv/t/plex-pros-2024/906029)) — *"an inside look at everything users can do on the Discover tab, plus an exclusive preview of what's next"* — but the recap blog ([Pro Week '24 TLDR](https://www.plex.tv/blog/plex-pro-week-24-tldr-back-to-the-fireside/)) is gated and returned 403 on fetch. Could not verify whether any new algorithm details were disclosed at that session.

There is no public Plex "RecSys-style" paper or engineering blog with model details (recall@k, candidate generation strategy, ranking features). All public information is marketing-tier.

## 6. Visible failure modes

Three categories of complaint dominate the public record.

### 6a. Discover Together privacy backlash (Nov–Dec 2023)

Easily the most documented failure. Sources: [TechHive](https://www.techhive.com/article/2157803/plex-discover-together-privacy-concerns.html), [404 Media](https://www.404media.co/plex-users-fear-discover-together-week-in-review-feature-will-leak-porn-habits-to-their-friends-and-family/), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2023/11/28/plex_privacy/), [PCGamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/plex-says-controversial-service-that-emails-your-anime-habits-to-your-mates-is-opt-in-actually-pointing-at-a-screen-where-you-still-have-to-turn-it-off/), [Cord Cutters News](https://cordcuttersnews.com/this-new-plex-feature-shares-your-weird-watch-history-with-friends-and-its-tricky-to-turn-off/), [Slashdot](https://yro.slashdot.org/story/23/11/27/1836216/plex-users-fear-new-feature-will-leak-porn-habits-to-their-friends-and-family), [Plex Forum thread](https://forums.plex.tv/t/discover-together-and-week-in-review-emails-are-a-massive-breach-of-privacy-and-trust/860302).

What happened: Discover Together's onboarding defaulted watch-history sharing to "Friends Only" rather than "Private", and **automatically promoted everyone who already had access to your personal Plex library into a "friend"**. Then "Week in Review" emails started landing in inboxes summarising what each friend had watched.

Verbatim user complaints from the Plex forum thread and 404 Media coverage:

- *"What the hell are people at Plex thinking. Automatically opting everyone in for sharing their watch history with friends?"* ([TechHive](https://www.techhive.com/article/2157803/plex-discover-together-privacy-concerns.html))
- *"I just got an email about a friend's watching habits which he definitely didn't want to share"* ([404 Media](https://www.404media.co/plex-users-fear-discover-together-week-in-review-feature-will-leak-porn-habits-to-their-friends-and-family/))
- *"I wonder how many people just had their week's porn selections emailed to their Plex friends"* ([404 Media](https://www.404media.co/plex-users-fear-discover-together-week-in-review-feature-will-leak-porn-habits-to-their-friends-and-family/))
- *"watch information, not even done directly from my server, is being distributed in such a cavalier manner … a MASSIVE invasion of privacy"* — forum user *dmurph* ([Plex Forum](https://forums.plex.tv/t/discover-together-and-week-in-review-emails-are-a-massive-breach-of-privacy-and-trust/860302))
- *"This is the kind of thing that can end friendships, tear apart families, result in getting fired or even lead to physical harm"* — *dmurph* ([Plex Forum](https://forums.plex.tv/t/discover-together-and-week-in-review-emails-are-a-massive-breach-of-privacy-and-trust/860302))
- *"these emails will finish that. It is the embodiment of enshitification of a good product"* — forum user *OttoKerner* ([Plex Forum](https://forums.plex.tv/t/discover-together-and-week-in-review-emails-are-a-massive-breach-of-privacy-and-trust/860302))
- *"The fact that this data is available to you AT ALL … That is just … Mind boggling, and completely against the very notion of self hosting"* ([404 Media](https://www.404media.co/plex-users-fear-discover-together-week-in-review-feature-will-leak-porn-habits-to-their-friends-and-family/))

Plex's official response (via spokesperson, reported by [TechHive](https://www.techhive.com/article/2157803/plex-discover-together-privacy-concerns.html)): *"many users became 'aware' of the feature for the first time last week when Plex sent out the activity emails"* and that some users *"may have clicked through these settings during the onboarding process without reading their selections."* Plex committed to redesigning the onboarding pages and decoupling library-sharing from social-friending.

**Relevance to HelpME2C:** social/group features that auto-enrol or auto-link from existing relationships are reputational dynamite. The defaults Plex picked were technically opt-in but practically opt-out, and the blowback was severe.

### 6b. "Recommending content you can't even watch" / availability mismatches

Sources: [Plex Forum: "Services configured but in Discover tab everything shows as no locations available"](https://forums.plex.tv/t/services-configured-but-in-discover-tab-everything-shows-as-no-locations-available/827292), [Plex Forum: "Not finding streaming content I know exists when I search in Plex"](https://forums.plex.tv/t/not-finding-streaming-content-i-know-exists-when-i-search-in-plex/899617), [Plex Forum: "Streaming Services not indicating that the content is available on their service"](https://forums.plex.tv/t/streaming-services-not-indicating-that-the-content-is-available-on-their-service/909242).

Typical complaint shape: user has configured Netflix + Disney+ + Max + Prime, opens a title in Discover that they know is on Netflix, sees *"There are no locations currently available for this title"*. Verified against JustWatch — content was in fact available. The forum thread from a Netherlands user (Sabiwa, Jan 2023) is representative. Affects multiple regions; UK catchup services are particularly flaky per a related forum topic.

This is a particularly acute failure mode for a "what to watch tonight" surface — the entire premise of Discover is *"only show me things I can actually press play on"*, and when the availability metadata is wrong the value proposition collapses.

### 6c. Ads, app redesign, and "polluting my library with online content"

Sources: [PCWorld: "Annoyed by the new Plex app? Change these 4 settings"](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2654717/annoyed-by-the-new-plex-app-change-these-4-settings.html), [AVS Forum: "Plex is now running ads before your ripped library"](https://www.avsforum.com/threads/plex-is-now-running-ads-before-your-ripped-library.3304812/), [Plex Forum: "Ads about my own content"](https://forums.plex.tv/t/ads-about-my-own-content/838109), [Trustpilot reviews aggregating to 1.4/5](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/plex.tv?page=2), [Slashdot on the Plex redesign](https://entertainment.slashdot.org/story/24/11/22/1757247/plexs-upcoming-app-redesign-is-a-big-swing-at-going-legit).

PCWorld's article exists *because* the failure mode is so common that there's an audience for a "how to switch this all off" tutorial. The four settings users want to disable: Live TV tab, On Demand tab, Discover tab, and User Reviews. From [PCWorld](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2654717/annoyed-by-the-new-plex-app-change-these-4-settings.html): users complain of *"buggy performance, missing features, and a cluttered, confusing interface"*.

Verbatim user reactions to the broader Plex direction:

- Movies interrupted "every 9 minutes for 1 minute 50 second advertisements"; one ~1h45 film took 2h30 to watch because of ad breaks ([Facebook user group, summarised in search](https://www.facebook.com/groups/609342120502134/posts/1766138301489171/), corroborated in [AVS Forum thread](https://www.avsforum.com/threads/plex-is-now-running-ads-before-your-ripped-library.3304812/)).
- Recurring complaint that *"Plex constantly pushes recommendations and content from online sources, making it impossible to distinguish between files on a personal server and files Plex is promoting from online sources"* (paraphrased from multiple Trustpilot reviews; representative reviews on [Trustpilot p.2](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/plex.tv?page=2)).

### 6d. The Watch Together removal

Sources: [Android Authority](https://www.androidauthority.com/plex-watch-together-removed-3529939/), [PCWorld](https://www.pcworld.com/article/2619590/plex-is-dropping-a-popular-feature-from:the-new-streaming-apps/), [Phil Tann coverage](https://www.philtann.com/computing/plex-announces-major-changes-remote-streaming-now-requires-subscription-watch-together-phased-out/), [Plex Forum: Watch Together going away in app](https://forums.plex.tv/t/watch-together-going-away-in-app/906895). 266+ replies on the forum thread alone — a popular feature being removed from native apps and surviving only on the web is a meaningful product signal: Plex is consolidating around Discover Together (social activity layer) and away from synchronous co-viewing.

### 6e. The broader identity crisis

Threaded through all the above is a single narrative: Plex started as **self-hosted media server software for power users**, pivoted (2022 onward) to **streaming aggregator + AVOD + social network**, and the original power-user base is unhappy. The Slashdot headline [*"Plex's Upcoming App Redesign is a Big Swing at Going Legit"*](https://entertainment.slashdot.org/story/24/11/22/1757247/plexs-upcoming-app-redesign-is-a-big-swing-at-going-legit) captures the pivot; the Trustpilot 1.4/5 captures the reception.

## Summary

File written to: `/Users/wouterschreuders/Code/HelpME2C/research/competitive-benchmark/raw/platform-plex-discover.md`. The strongest evidence is around the **Discover Together privacy backlash** (Nov 2023, multiple major outlets + verbatim forum quotes) and the **Vionlabs ML partnership** (April 2023, gives the only concrete handle on Plex's recommendation taxonomy: ~30 predicted genres, ~20 moods, ~1,200 keywords, scene-level fingerprints). The **algorithm itself is opaque** — there's no Plex equivalent of a Netflix Tech Blog post; everything is marketing-tier statements. **Cold-start onboarding** is also undercovered in public sources — no Plex-published screenshots of the picker flow are easy to find, and the support.plex.tv articles return 403 to WebFetch, so onboarding detail is reconstructed from third-party guides (HelloTech, HowToGeek, Gizmodo). The **biggest gap** is whether Discover ever does true cross-medium scoring (anime ↔ live-action ↔ doc) — public docs do not confirm or deny, and the Vionlabs taxonomy is medium-internal. **Group recommendations in the HelpME2C sense (multi-user joint scoring) do not exist on Plex** — Discover Together is a social activity feed with a friend-popularity hub, not a group-aware ranker, and the synchronous co-viewing product (Watch Together) is being sunset on most platforms as of Feb 2025.
